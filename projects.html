Credit Card Fraud Detection NN

Baseline dense neural network to flag rare fraudulent card transactions (â‰ˆ0.17% fraud rate).

Trains a small dropoutâ€‘regularized neural net on the public credit card fraud dataset (OpenML id 42175). It standardizes features, applies class weighting for imbalance, evaluates with AUC / Precisionâ€‘Recall focus, sweeps thresholds, calibrates probability quality, and projects hiddenâ€‘layer embeddings with UMAP to visualize risk clusters. Outputs readyâ€‘toâ€‘discuss artifacts (PNG charts) plus a recommended starting review threshold (max F1) that can be shifted toward higher precision (fewer alerts) or higher recall (more fraud caught).

Expected vs Observed Outcomes

  • Expectation: High ROC AUC (>0.95) but modest precision at balanced (0.50) threshold due to extreme rarity.
  • Observed: ROC AUC 0.9821; Average Precision 0.6844 (strong ranking ability).
  • Precision @0.50: 0.054 (alerts mostly benign at default cutoff).
  • Recall @0.50: 0.908 (most fraud is caught early).
  • Brier Score: 0.0211 (probabilities well calibrated for decision tiers / cost modeling).
  • Takeaway: Lower the threshold to chase maximum recall only if ops capacity tolerates higher false alert load; otherwise raise threshold slightly to boost alert purity, guided by the threshold sweep chart.
